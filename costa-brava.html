📅 Suggested 8-Night Itinerary

Day 1 — Thursday, July 23

✈️ Fly JFK → Barcelona - Depart JFK ~5:10 PM on AA66 - Overnight flight (sleep on the plane!)

Day 2 — Friday, July 24

🏙️ Arrive Barcelona — explore the city

  • Morning: Arrive BCN ~6:45 AM. Check into Miiro Borneta (early check-in or drop bags). Walk Las Ramblas, Boqueria Market, Gothic Quarter.
  • Afternoon: Relax, nap, rooftop pool — ease into the trip. Head to Sartoria Panatieri at 5:30 PM — excellent Italian/pizza in El Born.
  • Evening: After dinner, walk the Gothic Quarter at dusk. Cocktails on the hotel rooftop.

📍 Optional: Bar del Pla — excellent tapas & wine bar on Carrer de Montcada, 5-min walk from the hotel (tuna tataki, croquetas). Great for a late-night bite or post-dinner drinks on any Barcelona evening.

🏨 Stay: Miiro Borneta, Barcelona

Day 3 — Saturday, July 25

🏙️ Barcelona Day 2 — Gaudí & beach

  • Morning: Sagrada Familia (book tickets in advance!), Park Güell.
  • Afternoon: Barceloneta beach. Lunch at a chiringuito (beach restaurant).
  • Evening: Dinner at Paradiso at 7:00 PM (Carrer de Rera Palau 4, El Born) — hidden speakeasy-style cocktail bar behind a pastrami bar. €60 booked. Evening stroll through El Born.

📍 Don't miss: Try to visit La Cova Fumada in Barceloneta (Day 2 or 3) — a legendary no-frills tapas bar famous for its bomba (potato croquette). Cash only, no reservations, expect a queue but it moves fast. Open mornings/lunch only.

🏨 Stay: Miiro Borneta, Barcelona

Day 4 — Sunday, July 26

🚗 Pick up car & drive to Tossa de Mar

  • Morning: Sleep in. Last Barcelona morning — revisit a favourite spot or browse El Born shops.
  • Afternoon: Check out of Miiro Borneta. Enjoy Barceloneta beach or a long lunch. Store bags at hotel if needed.
  • Evening: Train or taxi to BCN Airport (~35 min). Pick up rental car at 10:30 PM.
  • Night: Drive to Tossa de Mar (~1 hr, easy Sunday night roads). Check into Elisabeth by the Sea late.

🏨 Stay: Elisabeth by the Sea, Tossa de Mar

Day 5 — Monday, July 27

🏖️ Tossa de Mar — explore & beaches

  • Morning: Explore the medieval walled town (Vila Vella). Walk up to the castle walls for panoramic views. Swim at Platja Gran below the walls.
  • Midday: Lunch in the Old Town.
  • Afternoon: Hike the Camí de Ronda coastal path to Cala Pola (beautiful secluded cove, ~30 min walk). Swim, snorkel, relax.
  • Evening: Seafood dinner at a beachfront restaurant. Sunset walk along the walls.

🏨 Stay: Elisabeth by the Sea, Tossa de Mar

Day 6 — Tuesday, July 28

🚗 Drive north to Baix Empordà (1 hr)

  • Morning: Drive north via the coastal road. Optional stop at Calella de Palafrugell for coffee & a stroll along the whitewashed waterfront.
  • Midday: Check into Mas de Torrent. Lunch at the hotel.
  • Afternoon: Pool & spa afternoon. Bike ride through olive groves if you're feeling active.
  • Evening: Drive to Peratallada (10 min) — romantic dinner in the medieval walled village.

🏨 Stay: Mas de Torrent

Day 7 — Wednesday, July 29

🏖️ Coves & villages — the best day

  • Morning: Drive to Begur → hike down to Sa Tuna cove (stunning!). Swim in turquoise water.
  • Midday: Lunch at Cap Sa Sal — Mediterranean cuisine with terrace overlooking the sea, near Aiguafreda cove in Begur. (Book in advance — popular in summer!)
  • Afternoon: Explore Pals medieval village (10 min from hotel), or return to hotel pool/spa.
  • Evening: Dinner at Mas de Torrent's gourmet restaurant. Stargazing from the terrace.

🏨 Stay: Mas de Torrent

Day 8 — Thursday, July 30

🚗 Drive to Girona (30 min) — city finale

  • Morning: Leisurely checkout from Mas de Torrent. One last walk through the gardens.
  • Midday: Drive to Girona. Check into Hotel Palau Fugit. Lunch at a café on Plaça de la Independència.
  • Afternoon: Explore Girona — cathedral, Jewish Quarter, colourful houses on the Onyar river. Rocambolesc (Roca brothers' ice cream!).
  • Evening: Celebration dinner — try for El Celler de Can Roca (book months ahead!) or one of Girona's excellent restaurants.

🏨 Stay: Hotel Palau Fugit, Girona

Day 9 — Friday, July 31

🚗 Day trip to Cadaqués & Cap de Creus (~1 hr each way)

  • Morning: Drive north via Figueres. Optional stop at the Dalí Theatre-Museum (1-2 hrs — surreal & unforgettable).
  • Midday: Arrive Cadaqués. Explore the whitewashed village — art galleries, harbour. Lunch at Compartir (from the El Bulli chefs!) or Casa Anita (legendary local spot).
  • Afternoon: Visit Salvador Dalí's house-museum at Portlligat (book in advance!). Drive to Cap de Creus lighthouse — Spain's easternmost point; wild, lunar landscapes. Optional swim at Cala Fredosa or Cala Jugadora.
  • Evening: Drive back to Girona. Farewell dinner. Sunset walk along the Onyar river.

🏨 Stay: Hotel Palau Fugit, Girona

🤔 Notes & Alternatives

Day Trip to Cadaqués (Day 9)

Cadaqués is about 1 hr from Girona via Figueres. It's a full but rewarding day — you can stop at the Dalí Theatre-Museum in Figueres on the way, then explore the village, visit Portlligat, and drive to Cap de Creus. Leave early to make the most of it. Book Dalí's house-museum tickets well in advance (limited capacity).

On Barcelona Hotels

We booked Miiro Borneta in El Born — rooftop pool, great location opposite Ciutadella Park, walkable to everything. Other options if plans change: - Hotel Serras Barcelona — Waterfront boutique, rooftop pool with marina views (€350–500) - Mercer Hotel Barcelona — Medieval palace in the Gothic Quarter; more intimate, plunge pool (€400–600) - Wittmore Hotel — Adults-only, hidden gem in the Gothic Quarter (€300–450)

Alternative Luxury Options Along the Route

🌤️ Late July Weather — Costa Brava

  • Average highs: 28–31°C (82–88°F) — slightly cooler than the Balearics!
  • Sea temperature: 23–25°C (warm enough for swimming)
  • Rain: Very rare; 1–2 brief afternoon showers possible
  • Sunset: ~9:20 PM (incredibly long golden evenings)
  • UV: High — bring sunscreen for beach/hiking days
  • ⚠️ Peak season — book restaurants in advance, arrive at beaches early

🍽️ Don't Miss — Food & Drink

  • Cap Sa Sal (Begur) — Mediterranean cuisine on a sea-view terrace near Aiguafreda cove. Spectacular setting for a special lunch. ⭐ Booked for Day 7
  • El Celler de Can Roca (Girona) — 3 Michelin stars, often #1 restaurant in the world. Book months ahead!

🗺️ Local Tips & Hidden Gems

Curated from local travel guides

Must-Visit Coves & Beaches (by area)

Near Tossa de Mar: - Cala Pola — Secluded turquoise cove, 30-min walk on Camí de Ronda from Tossa. Quieter than the main beach. Dramatic cliffs. - Cala Bona — Even more secluded, perfect for those seeking total quiet - Platja Gran — Main beach below the castle walls; ideal for sunbathing with a view

Near Begur / Baix Empordà (best beaches on the coast!): - Sa Tuna — Small fishing cove with a great restaurant (Sa Tuna Restaurant at Hotel Sa Tuna). Crystal water, rocky coastline. Top pick! - Aiguablava — Turquoise bay, easy swimming, one of the most photogenic spots on Costa Brava - Platja Fonda — Steep stairs down but worth it for crystal-clear water. Bring shade & snacks (no facilities) - D'Aigufreda — Walk from Sa Tuna along the coast; wonderful views the whole way - Tamariu — Tiny cove, great lunch spot, clear water - El Golfet, Canadell & Port Pelegrí (Calella de Palafrugell) — Series of small beaches ideal for swimming and snorkeling

Villages Not to Miss

  • Peratallada — Walled medieval village frozen in time; cobblestone streets, arched doorways. The entire village is a romantic dinner destination. ~10 min from Mas de Torrent.
  • Pals — Perfectly preserved medieval hill village with panoramic views; rice paddies below. Great independent shops and restaurants.
  • Calella de Palafrugell — White-washed fishing village with colourful boats pulled up on small beaches. Walk the Camí de Ronda coastal path to Llafranc.
  • Cadaqués (if extending to 8 days) — Salvador Dalí's whitewashed village at the tip of Cap de Creus. Artistic, bohemian, stunning. Worth the drive north.

Experiences & Activities

  • 🎨 Salvador Dalí Theatre-Museum (Figueres, 45 min from Girona) — Surreal, unforgettable. A must for art lovers.
  • 🌿 Marimurtra Botanical Garden (Blanes) — Stunning terraced gardens overlooking the coast; 16 hectares of Mediterranean and exotic flora.
  • 🏰 Begur Castle — 11th-century ruins atop the hill; panoramic 360° views of the coast. Best at sunset.
  • 🚶 Camí de Ronda — Ancient coastal patrol path; walk sections between coves (Sa Tuna → D'Aigufreda; Calella → Llafranc; Tossa → Cala Pola)
  • 🍷 Wine tasting — Empordà wine region; DO Empordà wineries near Peratallada/Pals
  • 🧀 Local markets — Begur (Wednesday), Palafrugell (Sunday & Tuesday), Girona (Saturday)

Driving Tips

  • Pick up the rental car in Barcelona (airport or city) — both are easy
  • The GI-682 coastal road from Tossa to Sant Feliu is one of Spain's most scenic drives (winding cliffs!)
  • Parking in small coastal towns can be challenging in July — arrive at beaches before 10 AM
  • Most coves have paid parking lots nearby (€5-8/day)
  • Begur to its coves (Sa Tuna, Aiguablava) = narrow winding roads; take it slow
  • Total driving for the trip is minimal — longest single segment is Barcelona → Tossa (1 hr on the AP-7 motorway)
